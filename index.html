// WHY I BUILT BHARAT MONITOR
01
India's governance data is scattered across 50+ portals. I wanted a single military-grade situational-awareness interface that unifies fiscal, defence, peace, environment, and social intelligence in real time.
02
Most dashboards serve stale numbers. I built a live AI backbone — every metric is freshly reasoned from the web, not hardcoded six months ago in a CSV file.
03
As an Indian builder, I believe every citizen deserves a transparent, military-grade view of their own country — its rivers, air, money, corruption and borders.
04
Inspired by worldmonitor.app, I proved the same intelligence-dashboard concept could be built for India's uniquely complex 28-state federal structure and $3.9T economy.
05
This is open civic tech — a statement that powerful geopolitical tools shouldn't be locked behind enterprise paywalls. Bharat Monitor is free, open, and built for Bharat.
